Before engaging with this framework, the reader must understand a foundational point: this is not a conventional attack on the Riemann Hypothesis. It does not proceed by the standard methods, and more critically, solving the prime distribution problem or locating zeros is not the goal of this work.

The conventional framing of RH asks: "Why do the non-trivial zeros of ζ(s) lie on the line Re(s) = ½?" This treats zeros as passive analytic objects to be located, and primes as the ultimate object of interest. Every major programme of the last 160 years — Hilbert-Pólya, Connes, Deninger — has pursued this framing. None has succeeded.

This framework argues that the framing itself was wrong.

The question driving this document is not about primes or zeros per se. It is:

What kind of thing is ζ(s), and where does its self-action physically reside?

RH — including the prime distribution it implies — becomes a corollary of correctly answering that question. Primes and zeros are not the destination. They are symptoms of a deeper structural identity waiting to be recognised.

The Reorientation in One Paragraph

The conventional view holds that zeros are passive objects sitting on (or near) the critical line, and that mathematics must find an external structure — an operator, a geometry, a symmetry — that forces them there. This document proposes the opposite: zeros are active agents that generate the field whose ground state is the critical line. The operator that confines them is not missing and was never external. It is ζ(s) acting on itself — an endomorphism — and the critical line is not a cage imposed from outside. It is the unique coherent ground state of a self-reacting variational system.

This shift transforms RH from a location problem into a stability problem, and ultimately into a variational problem whose answer is structurally identical to particle confinement in Yang-Mills gauge theory.
